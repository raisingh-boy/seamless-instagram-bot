### TITLE

Your Body Speaks a Language You Already Know

---

### DESCRIPTION

We tend to think language and movement are separate. Language is symbolic. Movement is physical.

But Carrión (2026) in Cognitive Linguistic Studies shows they are the same cognitive operation.

Two mechanisms.

First — conceptual metonymy. A part stands for the whole. When a dancer's hand reaches across space, it's not just biomechanics. It's intention, desire, connection — compressed into a gesture. Your brain reads it instantly with the same processing that lets you understand "the White House announced."

Second — conceptual metaphor. Two dancers in contact aren't just sharing space. Their interaction is structured as conversation, struggle, support. You don't need an explanation. Your body knows.

Mark Johnson writes: aesthetics is not a luxury. It is the very capacity to make meaning. Art is simply an exemplary manifestation of what every body does all the time.

A touch is never just a touch. Your body is constructing meaning with every degree of freedom.

📚 Carrión, O.B. (2026). Meaning Construal in Contemporary Dance. Cognitive Linguistic Studies, 13(1), 245–273. DOI: 10.1075/cogls.00147.car
